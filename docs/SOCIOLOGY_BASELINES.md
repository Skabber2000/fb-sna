# Ukrainian Population Sociological Baselines

**Purpose:** Quantified, citable benchmarks for evaluating the FB-SNA audience
against the general Ukrainian population. Compiled 2026-06-06 via web research
(agent-assisted). All figures carry source, year, and comparability caveats.
"Not found" items are flagged rather than estimated.

---

## 1. Education (tertiary/higher attainment)

| Metric | Value | Source | Year |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tertiary attainment, population aged ≥15 | **26.3%** | National statistics (8.45M of 32.08M) | 2020 |
| Tertiary gross enrollment ratio (NOT attainment) | ~83% | UNESCO UIS | recent |
| OECD reference average (25–64 tertiary) | 40% | OECD Education at a Glance | 2025 |

- Kyiv-specific tertiary share: **not found** in citable survey (Kyiv consistently most-educated region).
- Ukrainian FB users' education breakdown: **not found** — no published study.
- Caveat: 26.3% is ages ≥15 (depresses adult rate; working-age ~40–50% informally cited but unciteable).
  Enrollment ratio (83%) is a flow, not attainment — do not equate.

## 2. Values — WVS Wave 7 (Ukraine 2020, pre-war)

- Position: **high secular-rational**, **survival-leaning** moving toward self-expression;
  European Orthodox cluster (Bulgaria/Romania), outranking them on both axes.
- Wave 6 → 7 shifts: importance of God 64.0%→56.3%; obedience in children 42.0%→31.1%;
  religious faith in children 22.0%→14.9%.
- **No post-2022 WVS wave exists.** Values benchmarks predate the full-scale invasion — flag in any comparison.
- Sources: WVS W7 Ukraine (fieldwork 2020); UCEP national report; Inglehart-Welzel map.

## 3. Institutional trust & attitudes to the West (wartime)

Razumkov Centre, Feb–Mar 2025, n=2,018, MoE ±2.3% — trust balance (trust − distrust):

| Institution | Balance |
|---|---|
| Armed Forces of Ukraine | **+88.8%** |
| Volunteer detachments | +77.0% |
| Volunteer organizations | +65.8% |
| President | +26.3% |
| Government (Cabinet) | **−48.2%** |
| Verkhovna Rada | **−58.9%** |

Corroborating: AFU trust ~92% (KIIS 2024); Zelensky 58–74% across 2025 (Rating Group);
Rada ~20% trust / 76% distrust. EU membership support ~90%; NATO ~84% (KIIS);
80–90% Euro-Atlantic support across all regions.

**Key benchmark implication:** low gov/Rada trust in our audience = ALIGNED with population
(Rada net −59%). Distinctiveness shows on **army** and **EU/NATO** items, where the
population is overwhelmingly positive.

Caveat: wartime CATI surveys exclude occupied territories and most refugees; rally effect on army trust.

## 5. Antisemitism / xenophobia

- **ADL Global 100:** Ukraine antisemitic-attitudes index fell **46% (2019) → 29% (2023)** —
  largest decline in Eastern Europe, lowest score in regional set.
  Comparison 2023: Poland 35%, Hungary 37%, Russia 26%. Partly a "Zelensky effect."
- **Roma (Bogardus social distance, KIIS):** historically ~6.00/7 → ~5.08 (Sep 2022),
  ~5.37 (2023). Still the highest social distance of any group.
- Caveats: ADL = 11-stereotype index (attitude prevalence, not incidents); wartime
  mode-change may distort the drop. No recent Pew Ukraine number.

## 6. Social-media discourse quality baselines

- Platform-wide toxic-comment rate: **~1.27%** (182M of 14.3B comments; classifier-defined, 2025).
- Academic "uncivil comment" baselines: single-digit % to ~10% depending on definition.
- Public-figure pages attract concentrated abuse (UK/Twitter studies — not FB, not Ukraine).
- **No Ukrainian-FB public-figure baseline exists.** Treat our constructiveness/civility
  measurement as a novel metric; compare directionally against the 1–10% band.

## 7. "Intellectual elite" operationalization

- **Two-step flow / opinion leaders** (Katz & Lazarsfeld 1955, Decatur n=800):
  opinion leaders = credible domain advice-sources; operationalized by (a) self-designation,
  (b) sociometric nomination, (c) Childers 1986 6-item Opinion Leadership Scale.
- **Bourdieu cultural capital:** institutionalized (credentials) + embodied (discourse register)
  + objectified. "Intellectual elite" claim rests on credentials × register.
- **Defensible thresholds:** (1) tertiary share materially above ~26% (≥15) / ~40% (working-age);
  (2) high advice-giving / network centrality; (3) analytical discourse register.
- **No universal numeric cutoff exists** — the claim must be comparative, and two-step-flow
  influence ≠ Bourdieusian status; be explicit which is claimed.

## Cross-cutting warnings

1. Wartime sampling excludes occupied territories + refugees; CATI skews older/less mobile.
2. Values data is pre-war (2020); trust data is wartime. Do not blend.
3. Ukrainian Facebook skews female (~58%), urban, 25–34 largest (Statista/NapoleonCat 2024);
   Telegram now dominates usage — FB is a self-selecting subset. Benchmark against FB-user
   norms where possible, not just national.
4. Honest gaps: Kyiv tertiary share; FB-user education; numeric WVS coordinates;
   post-2022 values wave; UA-FB toxicity baseline; recent Pew antisemitism number.
